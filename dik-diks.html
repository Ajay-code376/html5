DIK-DIKS

The dik-dik is a type of dwarf antelope that lives in arid regions of East Africa.They can
live in such barren environments because they get water from the vegatation they eat.
They also take cover in undergrowth to protect themselves from predators,including
eagles and cats

There are four species of dik-diks,but just two of them live in Kenya.These include
Guenther's dik-dik and Kirk's dik-dik.Kirk's is the largest dik-dik,though it's still small
in comparison to other antelopes.It stands at just 45 centimetres(18 inches)tall and
weighs only 7.2 kilograms(15.8 pounds).Guenther's dik-dik is smaller but has a longer
snout.

The IUNC lists both Kirk's dik-dik and Guenther's dik-dik at least concern,though they are
threatened by expanding agricultural settlements and excessive hunting in some areas.
